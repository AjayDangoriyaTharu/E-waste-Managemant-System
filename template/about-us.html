Our Steps

How It’s Work

01

Waste Assessing

In the first step of e-waste management, "Waste Assessing," the focus is on comprehensively evaluating the types and quantities of electronic waste generated in a particular area. This involves conducting surveys, audits, and data collection to understand the scope of the e-waste problem and identify the most prevalent electronic devices contributing to it.

02

Start Recycling

The "Start Recycling" phase involves establishing efficient recycling systems and facilities equipped with advanced technologies. This step aims to responsibly process and extract valuable materials from e-waste, reducing its environmental impact. Collaboration with certified e-waste recyclers and government agencies is crucial to ensure safe and environmentally friendly disposal.

03

Better Together

The "Better Together" phase emphasizes the importance of community and industry collaboration. It involves educating the public about e-waste disposal practices, promoting collection drives, and fostering partnerships between businesses, NGOs, and government bodies. By working together, we can raise awareness and improve e-waste management on a larger scale.

04

Keep it Up Recycling

The final phase, "Keep It Up Recycling," underscores the need for continuous efforts in e-waste recycling. Sustainable practices and ongoing public awareness campaigns are vital to maintain the momentum and ensure that electronic waste is consistently recycled and not illegally disposed of. Regular updates and improvements in technology and recycling processes also play a crucial role in this phase.
